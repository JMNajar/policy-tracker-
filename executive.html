Who Controls Cannabis Regulation

Congressional legislation is only half the picture. These executive agencies shape cannabis policy through rules, guidance, and enforcement — often faster than Congress can act.

AgencyCannabis AuthorityWhy It Matters for Business
DEAScheduling decisions — Schedule I vs. III; hemp-derived cannabinoid classificationThe single biggest regulatory lever. Compound scheduling affects delta-8, delta-10, HHC, and THCO — not just plant cannabis. State legality varies by compound.
FDACannabis-derived ingredients in food, supplements, and medicineFDA rules set the B2B sales boundary for hemp CBD and THC products. Enforcement discretion changes without legislation.
DOJ / AGFederal prosecution prioritiesCan deprioritize cannabis enforcement without Congress. The AG's stance determines real-world federal risk.
Treasury / FinCENBanking guidance for cannabis businessesFinCEN memos tell banks whether they can serve cannabis clients. Changes here affect payment processing and capital access.
USDAHemp farming regulations, Farm Bill implementationSets THC threshold for hemp vs. cannabis classification. Affects what can be grown, tested, and sold across state lines.
IRS280E tax enforcement280E prevents cannabis businesses from deducting ordinary expenses. IRS posture is one of the largest cost factors for operators.
White House / PresidentExecutive orders, pardons, enforcement directionPresidential action moves faster than legislation. Pardons, enforcement memos, and agency directives can shift market conditions overnight.
